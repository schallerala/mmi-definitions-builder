\begin{dfn}
A word $x$ is called a \emph{prefix} of a word $w$ if $w = xy$ for some word $y$.
In particular, both $\epsilon$ and $w$ are prefixes of $w$.
A \emph{proper prefix} of $w$ is a prefix distinct from $\epsilon$ and $w$.
\end{dfn}
